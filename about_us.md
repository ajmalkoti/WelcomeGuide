## About CSIR-NGRI
The CSIR–National Geophysical Research Institute (CSIR–NGRI), Hyderabad, is a premier geoscientific research laboratory under the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR), Government of India. Established in 1961, the institute is dedicated to multidisciplinary research in Earth sciences, with a focus on understanding the structure, dynamics, and processes of the solid Earth system, natural resource exploration, and their societal applications. CSIR–NGRI conducts research that supports the sustainable utilization of geo-resources while enhancing resilience to natural hazards and environmental challenges. The institute contributes significantly to national development through scientific investigations related to resource exploration, environmental sustainability, and disaster risk mitigation.
The institute’s research activities are broadly organized under three major themes: Geodynamics, which focuses on understanding Earth processes and lithospheric evolution; Earthquake Hazards and Environmental Studies, addressing seismic hazards, landslides, groundwater quality, and climate-related challenges; and Natural Resources, emphasizing the exploration and sustainable management of groundwater, hydrocarbons, minerals, geothermal energy, and other geo-resources.
CSIR–NGRI possesses expertise across a wide range of advanced geoscientific disciplines, including seismology, gravity and magnetic studies, magnetotellurics, controlled-source seismics, gas hydrate investigations, geochemistry, geochronology, hydrochemistry, airborne geophysics, rock mechanics, and computational modeling of Earth processes. Supported by highly qualified scientists, technical and research personnel, advanced laboratories, state-of-the-art computational facilities, and nationwide observational networks, CSIR–NGRI continues to address emerging geoscientific challenges and deliver science-based solutions for societal benefit, sustainable development, and informed policy-making.
